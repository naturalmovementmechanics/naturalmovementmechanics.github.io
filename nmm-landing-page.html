Who this is for

Two common conditions.
Both very treatable.

Pelvic floor dysfunction is not something you manage. It's something you treat. This guide was designed for people living with two of the most common presentations — and who deserve access to real clinical education outside of a clinic.

  • You leak when you cough, sneeze, laugh, or jump
  • You feel heaviness or pressure in your pelvis
  • You've been told this is normal or just part of aging
  • You've never seen a pelvic floor PT — or can't access one
  • You're ready to actually do something about it
Stress Urinary Incontinence
Leaking when pressure increases suddenly.

Coughing, sneezing, laughing, jumping. The most common type of incontinence — and one of the most responsive to targeted technique training.

Pelvic Organ Prolapse
A feeling of heaviness, pressure, or descent.

When support structures weaken, symptoms include pelvic pressure and bulging. Graded, supervised rehabilitation is available — and often effective — at every severity level.
