Instructions

Complete this form honestly before your appointment and bring it with you. Each section covers one domain of pelvic health. Your responses will help your clinician understand your full symptom picture quickly and efficiently — saving time in the appointment for assessment and treatment. There are no wrong answers. Underreporting is the most common barrier to effective pelvic health care.

Clinical Action Tiers
Green · Monitor & Educate
All domains Green, or 1 domain Amber with no functional limitation. Focus: education, pelvic floor awareness, lifestyle optimization, preventive exercise. Consider 2–4 sessions to establish baseline and home program.
Amber · Structured Treatment
1–2 domains Amber, or 1 domain Red with functional limitation. Focus: targeted pelvic floor rehabilitation across flagged domains. Expect 6–12 sessions with progressive loading. Reassess at 6 weeks.
Red · Priority & Collaborative Care
2+ domains Red, or any domain Red with significant psychosocial burden. Focus: prioritize highest-impact domains, initiate multidisciplinary referrals as indicated, coordinate care. Reassess at 4 weeks.
Clinical Pattern Flags · Common Presentations to Recognize
Hypertonic / Overactive Pelvic Floor
Pelvic pain (esp. with sitting or intercourse) + urgency/frequency + constipation or straining + tailbone pain. Avoid kegel-first approach. Prioritize downtraining, breathing, manual therapy.
Hypotonic / Underactive Pelvic Floor
Stress leakage + prolapse heaviness + activity avoidance + low global impact score. Progressive loading, The Knack, breath coordination, functional positioning progression.
Mixed Presentation
Stress + urgency leakage + pelvic pain + bowel involvement. Assess tone first — mixed presentations often have a dominant component. Treat hypertonicity before adding strengthening.
Chronic Pelvic Pain / Central Sensitization
Pain 7+ NRS + 12+ months duration + multiple locations + high QoL burden + prior dismissal by providers. Prioritize therapeutic alliance, pain neuroscience education, graded exposure. Psychology referral strongly indicated.
